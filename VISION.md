# Kumbaja

## North Star
Kumbaja makes adversarial deliberation a standard part of the research workflow. The indie researcher who ships ideas fast can stress-test their thinking against a panel of heterogeneous agents before publishing — catching blind spots, surfacing hidden assumptions, and sharpening conclusions. "Run a kumbaja on it" becomes as routine as "run the tests."

## Who It's For

### The Indie Researcher
It's Tuesday morning. They've spent the weekend building something — a new architecture, a research prototype, a controversial take. They know their own reasoning has blind spots but don't have a lab full of colleagues to poke holes. They type `kumbaja run --topic "Is this approach sound?"` with a skeptic agent, a domain expert, and a user advocate. Ten minutes later they have a transcript of adversarial analysis — agreements, disagreements, unresolved tensions — that shows them what they missed. They fix the gaps before anyone else sees them.

## Principles
▸ Deliberation quality over feature surface — the ring must produce genuinely better outcomes than a single agent, or it doesn't ship
▸ Clinical rigor in every output — claims backed by evidence, disagreements surfaced precisely, output structured for inspection
▸ Sharp tool, not a platform — one thing well: running deliberation loops
▸ Composable by design — JSONL in, JSONL out, predictable exit codes, slots into any pipeline

## Direction
Toward human-in-the-loop deliberation. Agents surface the sharpest disagreements and unresolved tensions, then pause for human judgment on the hardest calls. The agents do the adversarial prep work; the human makes the final decision with full context. Kumbaja augments human judgment — it doesn't replace it.

## Identity

### Personality
▸ Clinical · rigorous · terse

### Voice
Reads like a lab report. Tables, metrics, evidence. No fluff, no enthusiasm, no apology. The deliberating agents argue; Kumbaja itself is neutral infrastructure.

### Emotional Register
Feels like instant peer review. Not warm, not cold — precise. The user finishes a deliberation feeling like their thinking has been genuinely tested, gaps surfaced, conclusions either strengthened or revised.

### Naming
▸ Names should be pronounceable, searchable, and not a pun.

## The Tension
Agent autonomy versus human judgment. "Run a kumbaja" like a test implies automation — fire and forget. But human-in-the-loop deliberation requires pausing, waiting, depending on human availability. The project's challenge is to make human-in-the-loop feel as effortless as automation, without sacrificing the judgment that makes it valuable.
